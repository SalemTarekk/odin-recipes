Plov

Description

Plov, also known as pilaf, is a fragrant rice dish originating from Central Asia and widely enjoyed in various cuisines. Typically featuring meat (such as lamb or chicken), onions, carrots, and aromatic spices, plov is cooked in a single pot, creating a flavorful and comforting one-dish meal. The rice absorbs the savory essence of the meat and spices, making it a popular and culturally significant dish often served during special occasions and celebrations.

ingredients

  • Rice
  • Meat (lamb, beef, or chicken)
  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Garlic
  • Vegetable oil or butter
  • Spices (cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, saffron)
  • Broth or water
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: Dried fruits and nuts (raisins, apricots, almonds, pine nuts)

instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Cut the meat into chunks.
  3. Slice or chop onions.
  4. Slice carrots into thin strips or grate them.
  5. Mince or crush garlic.
  6. If using, prepare dried fruits and nuts.
  7. If using, prepare dried fruits and nuts.
  8. Heat oil or butter in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  9. Add the meat chunks and brown them on all sides.
  10. Add sliced or chopped onions to the pot and sauté until translucent.
  11. Add minced or crushed garlic and sauté for an additional minute.
  12. Stir in the spices (cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, saffron) to coat the meat and onions.
  13. Add sliced carrots to the pot and sauté for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  14. Mix in the rinsed rice and sauté for a couple of minutes until the rice is well-coated with the other ingredients.
  15. Pour in broth or water, enough to cover the rice and meat. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  16. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. This may take about 15-20 minutes.
  17. If using, add dried fruits and nuts during the last few minutes of cooking.
  18. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork.
  19. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  20. Serve the plov hot, with additional garnishes if desired.
